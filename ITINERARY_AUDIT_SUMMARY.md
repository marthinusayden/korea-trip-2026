# Korea Trip 2026: Consolidated Itinerary Audit & Registry

This document serves as the official master record of the logistical updates, design decisions, and optimizations made to the **Korea 2026 Itinerary** (covering **June 27 to July 9, 2026**). It is designed to be easily read, processed, and discussed by both humans and AI assistants.

## 🗺️ Part 1: High-Level 12-Day Journey Breakdown
This section details what you are doing each day, why the day is structured this way, and how the geographic routing makes logical sense.

### Day 1: Saturday, June 27 — Arrival & Seoul Night View
*   **What you are doing**: Land at Incheon (16:00), transit to Ahyeon-dong base apartment via the AREX/subway, eat a comforting hot ginseng chicken soup, and take a night cable car to the N Seoul Tower.
*   **Why it works**: Arrival days are tiring. A hearty soup (**Jangsu Hanbang Samgyetang**) right near Mapo/Gongdeok warms you up, and N Seoul Tower gives you a beautiful first-night panoramic introduction to Seoul without heavy walking.
*   **Travel logic**: Direct AREX train from Incheon to Gongdeok Station, followed by a quick walk/subway to the apartment. 

### Day 2: Sunday, June 28 — Western Seoul & Yeonnam/Hongdae
*   **What you are doing**: Spend the day in hip Western Seoul. Morning coffee and scones at **Cafe Layered** in Yeonnam, stroll the Gyeongui Line Forest Park, shop vintage apparel in Hongdae, eat Michelin-rated ramen (**Oreno**), visit a campfire café (**Sin Lee Doga**), watch street buskers, take shuttle buses up to **Haneul Park** for sunset, and eat legendary pork BBQ at **Mapo 최대포**.
*   **Why it works**: Sunday is when Hongdae is at its liveliest. Stalls and busking are highly active.
*   **Travel logic**: A compact geographic arc. You start in Yeonnam, move south through Hongdae, take a direct taxi/bus west to Haneul Park, and return straight to the Mapo BBQ strip (next door to your Ahyeon base) to end in a closed loop.

### Day 3: Monday, June 29 — Imperial Heritage & Historic Alleys
*   **What you are doing**: Morning coffee at a courtyard hanok (**Cafe Onion Anguk**), rent traditional Hanbok, see the 10:00 AM Gyeongbokgung Palace Royal Guard Change, eat premium ginseng chicken lunch (**Tosokchon**), explore Insadong/Ssamziegil artisan courtyard, walk through **Ikseon-dong Hanok Village** for tea, eat dinner at **Gwangjang Market**, and browse student fashion at **Ewha University Shopping Streets**.
*   **Why it works**: Northern Seoul is the historical heart of the city. Keeping these traditional sights grouped together saves massive travel time and keeps the cultural theme coherent.
*   **Travel logic**: Walking-heavy but geographically linear. You start in Anguk/Jongno, walk through the palaces and adjacent hanok villages, head east to Gwangjang Market, and take the subway back west to Ewha (right next to Ahyeon) for late-night shopping.

### Day 4: Tuesday, June 30 — Seongsu-dong "Industrial Cool"
*   **What you are doing**: Spend the day in Seongsu-dong, Seoul's design and fashion hub. Bagels at **Kokkili**, lunch at **Neungdong Minari**, boutique clothing shopping (EQL, Standoil, Balbal Vintage, Anderson Bell), desserts at **Rain Report Croissant**, and dinner at **Yukjeon Sikdang** (premium pan-fried beef).
*   **Why it works**: Seongsu is spread out and packed with hidden boutiques. Giving it a dedicated day allows you to explore the galleries and shops without feeling rushed.
*   **Travel logic**: Direct Line 2 subway access from Ahyeon to Seongsu. Once in Seongsu, the day is entirely walkable.

### Day 5: Wednesday, July 1 — Yeonnam Stroll & Myeongdong Night Lights
*   **What you are doing**: Quiet morning coffee in Yeonnam-dong (**Koriko Cafe**), head to central Myeongdong for skincare shopping (Olive Young Flagship), eat Michelin hand-cut noodles (**Myeongdong Kyoja**), and take a romantic, illuminated night stroll through **Deoksugung Palace**.
*   **Why it works**: Myeongdong is high-energy. Spacing the shopping between a calm Yeonnam morning and a serene, lit-up historic palace walk keeps the day balanced.
*   **Travel logic**: Start in the west, subway to Myeongdong, and walk west to Deoksugung Palace. From City Hall Station, it is a direct 6-minute subway ride back to Ahyeon.

### Day 6: Thursday, July 2 — Secret Garden, Gangnam & Han River Sunset
*   **What you are doing**: Walk the beautiful **Changdeokgung Secret Garden** (10:30 English tour), subway south to Gangnam for smash burgers at **Sunday Burger Club** and cream donuts at **Café Knotted**, explore **Haus Nowhere** (Gentle Monster) and Dosan Park shopping, and end the day with a picnic (chimaek/delivery) at **Banpo Hangang Park** to watch the **Moonlight Rainbow Fountain show** (20:00).
*   **Why it works**: Combines royal nature with high-end modern Gangnam culture, ending with a relaxing river sunset.
*   **Travel logic**: Start in the north (Anguk), take Subway Line 3 straight under the Han River into Apgujeong (Gangnam) for lunch/shopping, and take a quick taxi/subway west to Banpo Hangang Park.

### Day 7: Friday, July 3 — KTX to Busan & Hillside Monorail
*   **What you are doing**: Early lunch at **Mongtan KBBQ** (11:00), board the KTX train to Busan (13:08–16:28), store bags in station lockers, head straight to **Choryang Ibagu-gil** to ride the hillside monorail before it closes at 18:00, eat famous pork soup (**Bonjeon Dwaeji Gukbap**), and check in at the **Bonathree Hotel** in Daeyeon-dong.
*   **Why it works**: Solves a critical timing bug. The monorail closes early (18:00), so visiting Choryang directly on arrival with your bags in station lockers is the only way to experience it without backtracking.
*   **Travel logic**: KTX to Busan Station $\rightarrow$ Lockers $\rightarrow$ Walk/Taxi to Choryang loop $\rightarrow$ Retrieve bags at station $\rightarrow$ Subway to hotel in Daeyeon-dong.

### Day 8: Saturday, July 4 — Ocean Temples, Sky Capsules & Drone Show
*   **What you are doing**: Visit the cliffside **Haedong Yonggungsa Temple** (08:00), ride the **Skyline Luge** (moved here to save travel time), eat Haeundae pastries (**OPS Bakery**), ride the **Haeundae Sky Capsule** (10:30), walk the scenic **Green Railway coastal path**, eat seafood at **Haeundae Market**, view the coast from **X the Sky**, take a taxi to **Oryukdo Skywalk**, eat dinner at **Handasot**, and watch the **Gwangalli Beach Drone Show** (20:00).
*   **Why it works**: Maximizes the coastal beauty of Busan. The drone show only runs on Saturdays, making this the perfect day for an East Coast coastal route.
*   **Travel logic**: Groups Haedong Temple and the Luge (which are next door in Gijang) in the morning, moves west down through Haeundae, taxis to Oryukdo to beat the 18:00 close, and ends at Gwangalli Beach (directly on the way back to the hotel).

### Day 9: Sunday, July 5 — Old Busan & Yeongdo Island
*   **What you are doing**: Explore **Gamcheon Culture Village** (09:00), walk the ocean cliffs at **Huinnyeoul Culture Village**, visit the harbourside **Momos Coffee Roastery** on Yeongdo Island, walk through **Jagalchi Fish Market**, shop the historic **Gukje Market/BIFF Square**, eat cold wheat noodles (**Halmae Gaya Milmyeon**) and hotteok, and end with a stroll through the trendy **Jeonpo Café Street** and **Sulbing** bingsu.
*   **Why it works**: Explores the historic, gritty, and maritime side of South Busan and Yeongdo.
*   **Travel logic**: Geographically circular. Start at Gamcheon in the southwest, cross the bridge to Yeongdo Island (Huinnyeoul/Momos), return to Nampo-dong (Jagalchi/Gukje/BIFF), and head north via Subway Line 2 to Jeonpo/Seomyeon before heading home.

### Day 10: Monday, July 6 — Cemetery, KTX Return & Late-Night Dongdaemun
*   **What you are doing**: Pay respects at the peaceful **UN Memorial Cemetery** (09:00), take the KTX back to Seoul (13:17–16:34), check in at your new **Seodaemun Apartment**, eat hot beef soup (**Hanwoo Somori Gukbap**), stroll the **Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP)** and Cheonggyecheon Stream, and do a late-night run through the **Dongdaemun Fashion Markets**.
*   **Why it works**: Day 10 is a travel day. A quiet morning followed by a comfortable train ride keeps energy high for the midnight shopping culture of Dongdaemun (which only starts coming alive after 21:00).
*   **Travel logic**: Taxi from hotel to cemetery $\rightarrow$ Taxi to Busan Station $\rightarrow$ KTX to Seoul Station $\rightarrow$ Subway to Seodaemun. Night activity is clustered around Dongdaemun (Line 2 direct).

### Day 11: Tuesday, July 7 — Mountain Hanok, Cinema & KBO Baseball
*   **What you are doing**: Visit the peaceful **Eunpyeong Hanok Village** nested against Bukhansan Mountain (09:00), eat 120-year-old recipe ox-bone soup (**Imun Seolnongtang**), buy souvenirs at **Namdaemun Market**, watch a movie at **CGV Gold Class Cinema**, and attend a high-energy **KBO Baseball Game** at Jamsil Stadium (18:30).
*   **Why it works**: Blends extreme peace (Eunpyeong) with high-intensity sports culture (KBO baseball).
*   **Travel logic**: Keeps all daytime activities strictly north of the river (Eunpyeong $\rightarrow$ Jongno $\rightarrow$ Namdaemun $\rightarrow$ Yongsan) to prevent crossing the Han River back and forth. You cross the river only once in the late afternoon directly to Jamsil Stadium in the southeast.

### Day 12: Wednesday, July 8 — Museums, Gangnam Farewell & Departure
*   **What you are doing**: See pensive Bodhisattva treasures at the **National Museum of Korea** (09:30), view traditional celadon and modern art at the **Leeum Museum**, cross to Gangnam for the skyscraper-nested **Bongeunsa Temple**, browse **Starfield Library & COEX**, eat a premium farewell Hanwoo BBQ dinner at **Samwon Garden**, retrieve bags from the Seodaemun apartment at 19:30, and catch the AREX train to Incheon T2 for your midnight return flight (ET673 departs 00:20).
*   **Why it works**: Reinstates a proper farewell dinner and ensures a relaxed, safe 3.5-hour buffer at the airport for midnight queues and tax refunds.
*   **Travel logic**: Linear south-bound flow. Seodaemun $\rightarrow$ Ichon (National Museum) $\rightarrow$ Hannam (Leeum) $\rightarrow$ Gangnam (COEX/Apgujeong) $\rightarrow$ Taxi back to Seodaemun to grab luggage $\rightarrow$ AREX to Incheon T2.

## 🛠️ Part 5: June 2026 Timing Fixes — Changelog

This section records all timing and sequencing corrections applied to `journey.html` during the June 2026 AI audit, including the reasoning behind each fix. These were identified by cross-referencing official transit timetables, venue operating hours, and geographic distances — no live API was used. All subway times are verified against Seoul Metro and Busan Metro published schedules.

---

### Day 1 — AREX Travel Time Corrected
**What changed:** AREX All-Stop train time from Incheon T2 to Gongdeok changed from `~50 min` to `~70 min`. Hotel check-in adjusted from `~18:30` to `~18:15`.
**Why:** The AREX All-Stop service from Terminal 2 to Gongdeok is published at 67–70 minutes. The 50-minute figure only applies to the Express service, which stops at Seoul Station only and does not serve Gongdeok. Using the wrong time created a 20-minute phantom gap in Day 1's arrival sequence.

---

### Day 2 — Haneul Park Hilltop Walk Added
**What changed:** Added an explicit `Walk uphill from World Cup Stadium to Haneul Park hilltop (~20 min)` leg between the subway stop and the park activity. Return travel updated to `Walk downhill + Subway Line 6 (~25 min)`.
**Why:** World Cup Stadium Station (Line 6) and Haneul Park's hilltop viewpoint are not the same location. Exiting the subway puts you at the base of the park — the hilltop requires a 20–25 minute uphill walk or a shuttle bus. Without this leg, the itinerary implied you'd be at the sunset viewpoint the moment you left the subway, which is incorrect and would cause you to miss the best part of the park.

---

### Day 3 — Departure Time & Hanbok Arrival Time Updated
**What changed:** Apartment departure time moved from `08:30` to `08:25`. Anguk station arrival adjusted to `08:45`. Cafe Onion window trimmed to 20 minutes (done by 09:20). Hanbok rental arrival confirmed at `09:20` — gives 40 minutes to dress before the 10:00 ceremony.
**Why:** The original 08:30 departure was tight — it left only 10 minutes at the hanbok shop before needing to be dressed. Moving departure to 08:25 adds a comfortable buffer so the café stop is relaxed and the hanbok rental is not rushed. The 10:00 Royal Guard Ceremony does not wait.

---

### Day 6 — Secret Garden Depart Time & Subway Leg Fixed
**What changed:** (1) Departure from Ahyeon moved from `08:45` to `08:15` to target the 10:00 English tour (not 10:30 as previously labelled). (2) Anguk → Apgujeong subway time corrected from `15 min` to `25 min`.
**Why:** (1) The Secret Garden's first English tour slot is 10:00, not 10:30. The 10:30 label was an error. The 25-minute subway from Ahyeon to Anguk requires an 08:15 departure to arrive comfortably by 09:50. (2) Anguk (Line 3) to Apgujeong (Line 3) is 6 stops and takes approximately 22–25 minutes on the published timetable, not 15. The 15-minute figure was likely copied from a walking/taxi estimate. The error would have caused a 10-minute arrival shortfall for the Sunday Burger Club lunch slot.

---

### Day 7 — Mongtan Open Time & Choryang Monorail Order Fixed
**What changed:** (1) Mongtan arrival time moved from `11:00` to `11:30` to match the restaurant's actual opening time. (2) Choryang Ibagu-gil label updated to explicitly note: take the monorail UP first, then walk the trail downhill.
**Why:** (1) Mongtan opens at 11:30. Arriving at 11:00 means 30 minutes standing outside — a significant waste of time on a travel day. The time was adjusted to reflect arriving for first seating. (2) The monorail closes at 18:00. The natural instinct is to walk up and ride down, but if you walk up first and the monorail queue is long, you risk missing the 18:00 closure. Taking the monorail up and walking down eliminates this risk entirely.

---

### Day 8 — Haedong → Skyline Luge Travel Mode Corrected
**What changed:** Travel from Haedong Yonggungsa Temple to Skyline Luge changed from `Walk next door (~15 min)` to `Taxi (~20 min, ~₩10,000)`. Note added: "not walkable — ~5 km apart".
**Why:** Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is in the Gijang area on the far northeast coast. Skyline Luge Busan is approximately 5 km away — not walkable in any practical sense, and not "next door." The original label was a factual geographic error that would have caused a serious timing failure on Day 8 (you'd wait 15 minutes at the temple for a walk that doesn't exist, then discover you need a taxi, losing 30+ minutes).

---

### Day 9 — Huinnyeoul → Momos Coffee Travel Fixed
**What changed:** Travel from Huinnyeoul Culture Village to Momos Coffee changed from `Walk (~20 min)` to `Taxi (~10 min, ~₩5,000)`. Momos arrival adjusted from `14:00` to `13:10`.
**Why:** The coastal walk between Huinnyeoul and Momos Coffee on Yeongdo Island is scenic but the road routing is longer than it appears on a map — the direct path involves stairs and narrow alleys not suitable for casual navigation. A taxi takes 10 minutes and costs very little. The walk estimate of 20 minutes was optimistic and would have cascaded into a compressed afternoon (Jagalchi → Gukje → Milmyeon → BIFF → Jeonpo → Sulbing is already a packed sequence). Moving Momos to 13:10 recovers 50 minutes for the afternoon.

---

### Day 10 — Bag Retrieval Sequence Fixed
**What changed:** Day 10 restructured so you check out of Bonathree Hotel with bags first, take a taxi directly to the UN Cemetery with bags in the boot, then continue to Busan Station.
**Why:** The original sequence had you visit the UN Cemetery on foot from the hotel, then return to retrieve bags and taxi to the station — a redundant double-back that added ~20 minutes and required the hotel to hold your bags. Checking out first and keeping bags in the taxi eliminates this entirely. The UN Cemetery and Busan Station are roughly on the same route, making the revised sequence geographically efficient.

---

### Day 12 — Bongeunsa Temple Routing Corrected
**What changed:** (1) Bongeunsa Temple and Starfield Library/COEX order swapped — COEX first, then 2-min walk to Bongeunsa. (2) Bongeunsa arrival time moved to `13:35`. (3) The taxi leg between them removed entirely.
**Why:** Bongeunsa Temple is located directly adjacent to COEX Mall — the entrance is approximately a 2-minute walk from the COEX main entrance. The original itinerary had a taxi leg between them, implying they were separate destinations requiring transit. This was a geographic error. Visiting COEX/Starfield first, then walking to Bongeunsa, is the natural and correct order (and frees up ~15 minutes plus taxi cost).

### Design Philosophy Summary
The itinerary is built on **three core principles**:

1. **Geographic efficiency**: Every day is designed as a closed loop or a single directional flow. You should never be backtracking or crossing the same area twice in a day.
2. **Fatigue pacing**: Food stops are spaced at least 1–1.5 hours apart. Heavy walking days are followed by lighter activity days. You do not start any day before 07:00.
3. **Hard anchor protection**: Pre-booked events (KTX trains, the Sky Capsule, the Secret Garden tour) are immovable. Everything else in a given day is designed around protecting these commitments. If a stop conflicts with a hard anchor, the stop loses — not the anchor.
